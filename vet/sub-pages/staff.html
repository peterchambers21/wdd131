Meet Our Staff

Dr. Emily Hart

Lead Veterinarian

Dr. Emily Hart brings over 15 years of hands-on experience in both small animal care and large animal medicine. As our Lead Veterinarian, she not only leads our clinical team but also shapes the compassionate philosophy that guides our practice. Dr. Hart has a particular passion for internal medicine and wellness care, helping animals live long, healthy lives through proactive, personalized treatment plans.
Clients often describe her as calm, intuitive, and deeply committed to every patient — whether she’s treating a beloved family pet or managing a herd health check on a local farm. Outside of work, she volunteers with local animal rescues and enjoys hiking New England trails with her two rescue dogs, Tilly and Cedar.

Dr. Marcus Wells

Large Animal Specialist

Raised on a cattle ranch in western Massachusetts, Dr. Marcus Wells combines firsthand agricultural knowledge with advanced veterinary training to serve our large animal clients. With a calm presence and practical approach, Dr. Wells is the go-to vet for farmers and ranchers in the region. He specializes in equine care, bovine herd management, and livestock emergency response.
Dr. Wells is known for his ability to build strong relationships with both animals and their owners. His background in ranching allows him to understand the daily challenges faced in the field, and he’s always willing to roll up his sleeves — whether it’s assisting with a difficult calving or performing a complex dental float. In his free time, Marcus enjoys horseback riding and mentoring future rural veterinarians.

Dr. Sophie Lin

Small Animal Surgeon

With a precise hand and a compassionate heart, Dr. Sophie Lin has earned a reputation as one of the most meticulous small animal surgeons in the region. She specializes in both routine and advanced procedures, including soft tissue and orthopedic surgeries, and takes the time to walk pet owners through every step of the process.
Dr. Lin graduated top of her class from Cornell’s veterinary program and has since completed additional surgical fellowships, but what sets her apart is her bedside manner — she's known for checking in after hours and treating every patient like family. When she’s not in surgery, she enjoys watercolor painting, cooking, and spending time with her cat Mochi (a three-legged survivor of one of her earliest cases).

Noah Alvarez

Veterinary Technician

Noah Alvarez is a cornerstone of our clinic — always in motion, always helpful, and always putting animals first. As a certified Veterinary Technician, Noah supports both surgical procedures and routine care, assists in diagnostics, and helps keep pets comfortable during stressful visits. His calm energy and intuitive understanding of animal behavior make him especially effective with nervous or reactive pets.
Originally from Rhode Island, Noah grew up rescuing strays and shadowing local vets, which led to his passion for hands-on care. He’s known for his “gentle giant” demeanor, quick sense of humor, and a knack for remembering every patient’s name — and their favorite treat. Outside the clinic, he volunteers at a wildlife rehabilitation center and is an amateur guitar player.

Ava Thompson

Client Care Coordinator

If you've called or walked into our clinic, chances are Ava Thompson has already made your day better. As our Client Care Coordinator, Ava is the heart of our front office — scheduling appointments, answering questions, and making sure every pet parent feels welcome and confident about their visit. Her background in customer service and animal care helps bridge the gap between clinical staff and clients.
Ava brings warmth, clarity, and attention to detail to every interaction. She's the type to remember your dog’s birthday, send handwritten thank-you notes, and keep things running smoothly on even the busiest days. When she's not at the desk, Ava enjoys baking, yoga, and spending time with her senior Labrador, Benny.

Liam Bennett

Mobile Services Assistant

Liam Bennett is our right-hand man on the road. As the Mobile Services Assistant, Liam helps coordinate and deliver veterinary care to farms, rural homes, and hard-to-reach properties. From organizing supplies and supporting procedures to helping wrangle goats and hold horses steady, he’s ready for anything — and always with a smile.
Originally interested in wildlife biology, Liam found his calling in mobile vet work where he gets to combine his love of animals with his problem-solving skills. He’s especially skilled with large animals and farm dogs, and clients often say he's just as good with people as he is with animals. When not working, Liam enjoys photography, camping, and restoring old farm equipment.
